Meet Layla 
Born in 2022, Layla—affectionately known as Luli—is an absolute light. She rocks her very own pair of pink glasses and is completely obsessed with her favorite children's book, A Flat for Rent (Dira Lehaskir). To her mothers, Tamar and Eden, she is perfect.
But when Layla was just five months old, Tamar and Eden sensed that something wasn't quite right. They embarked on an exhausting medical journey, searching for answers. After all standard tests came back completely normal, they were referred for extensive genetic sequencing.
When the results arrived, their reality changed forever. Layla was diagnosed with Xia-Gibbs Syndrome (XGS)—an ultra-rare, non-hereditary genetic condition caused by a disruption in the AHDC1 gene.
Today, Project Luli is driven by fierce love and a refusal to just wait for a miracle. We are racing against time, driving the breakthrough research needed to find one.

The Science & XGS
Headline:
Understanding Xia-Gibbs Syndrome (XGS)


Body Text: Xia-Gibbs Syndrome (XGS) is an ultra-rare genetic condition caused by mutations in the AHDC1 gene. The AHDC1 protein is believed to play a crucial role in key interactions within the cell nucleus, regulating gene expression and maintaining chromatin structure. When a mutation disrupts this gene, it leads to a complex array of developmental delays, hypotonia, and other physical anomalies.
Because this condition was only recently discovered, it is incredibly rare. As of May 2024, there are only 179 registered cases in the global patient registry across 33 countries, with just 4 known cases in Israel.
Currently, there is no cure and no specific therapeutic strategy for XGS. We are determined to change that.

The Research & Our Partners
Headline:
Pioneering the Path to a Cure


Body Text: We are not waiting for a miracle; we are funding the science to create one. Project Luli is actively collaborating with top-tier scientists to understand the pathogenic mechanisms of AHDC1 mutations and to discover viable therapeutic options.


Our Scientific Partners:
* Prof. Miguel Weil: A leading researcher in the Department of Cell Research and Immunology at Tel Aviv University, Prof. Weil brings extensive expertise in the molecular signaling pathways of the developing nervous system, neurodegenerative diseases, and the neuronal differentiation of stem cells. For Project Luli, he is spearheading a breakthrough initiative to develop a phenotypic assay for drug screening. 
* Dr. Lior Dor: A research associate at ALYN PARC currently investigating personalized approaches for advancing treatment for patients diagnosed with rare neurological conditions. He has a PhD in Animal Sciences from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem. Dr. Dor completed a postdoctoral research fellowship at Tel Aviv University, studying induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSC) to model rare neurological disorders with Prof. Miguel Weil and Prof. Gad Vatin. Dr. Dor is currently partnering with our team to push the boundaries of genetic research and clinical application for XGS.
